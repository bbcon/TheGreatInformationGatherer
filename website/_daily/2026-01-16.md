**Markets extended their broadening rally with small caps outperforming for a tenth consecutive session, while the Fed pivot toward an extended pause gained momentum as strong labor data reduced rate cut expectations.**

**Equities & Earnings**

Equity markets continued their rotation toward smaller names, with the Russell 2000 gaining 7.76% year-to-date and outperforming the S&P 500 for ten straight sessions - the longest streak since 1990.

- Equal-weight S&P 500 reached all-time highs on its sixth consecutive day of gains
- Small cap earnings revisions rose 25% since mid-May versus 14% for the S&P 500
- Memory chip stocks rallied 5%+ on AI infrastructure optimism, led by Micron and SanDisk
- TSMC shares gained 1.5% after initial 2.5% jump on blowout earnings and capex guidance of $52-56 billion for 2026
- Wall Street trading revenue hit a record $134 billion, supporting investment banking momentum

Financial sector performance diverged sharply, with capital markets beneficiaries outperforming credit-exposed names.

- Goldman Sachs and Morgan Stanley gained 4-8% on trading strength
- JPMorgan fell 6% on credit card exposure concerns
- Regional banks favored over money center banks on Main Street recovery expectations

**Fed & Monetary Policy**

The Federal Reserve's extended pause gained credibility as labor market strength reduced urgency for rate cuts.

- Jobless claims fell unexpectedly, remaining below 200,000
- Unemployment held steady at 4.4% - stable but showing gradual weakening
- JPMorgan and Santander now forecast zero Fed cuts in 2026, with potential hikes in 2027
- Market pricing remains around two cuts for the year
- Fed officials unanimously signaled pre-blackout that policy is "well-positioned with no urgency to move"

Front-end Treasury yields rose on the strong labor data, while the 10-year yield held steady around 417-418 basis points despite fiscal concerns.

**International Markets**

China implemented targeted monetary easing while regulatory pressures weighed on sentiment.

- PBOC cut structural lending rates 25 basis points to 1.25%, effective January 19
- Added 500 billion yuan in re-lending facilities for agriculture and small enterprises  
- New loan growth declined year-over-year in 2025 despite December uptick, reflecting weak borrower demand
- Chinese regulators ordered brokers to remove high-frequency trading servers from commodity exchange data centers, impacting major trading firms
- Trip.com collapsed over 30% in three days on antitrust probe into exclusive merchant contracts

The yuan strengthened to 7.0078 versus the dollar as officials signaled tolerance for 2-3% appreciation to ease trade tensions.

**Trade & Geopolitics**

The Trump administration advanced multiple trade initiatives while addressing domestic affordability concerns.

- Taiwan-US trade deal cut tariffs to 15% in exchange for $250 billion semiconductor investment
- TSMC indicated advanced technology will remain 1+ years ahead in Taiwan despite US expansion
- China's 2024 trade surplus reached a record $1.2 trillion with exports up 5.5% despite tariffs
- Supreme Court ruling on tariff validity expected Tuesday-Wednesday next week, coinciding with Trump's Davos speech

**Policy & Regulation**

Bipartisan pressure mounted on corporate sectors through affordability initiatives.

- Emergency 15-year power auction pushed to force Big Tech funding of electricity generation
- Residential electricity prices reached ~20 cents per kWh, up from 8-10 cents in the early 2000s
- Energy subsidy mandates totaling $15 billion in power plant funding target hyperscaler margins
- Housing policy proposals include 401(k) withdrawal provisions for down payments

**Fixed Income & Credit**

Corporate credit spreads tightened to 103 basis points - the tightest since June 2007 - prompting complacency warnings from Aberdeen and PIMCO.

- 2s10s Treasury spread flattened over 12 basis points in January to around 60 basis points
- Record corporate issuance expected, driven by utilities and AI infrastructure needs
- Investors favored short-to-intermediate maturities over long-end volatility
- European rates offered attractive pickup on hedged basis with minimal ECB cuts priced

**Commodities**

- Gold held near record highs above $4,600 per ounce, though Mark Mobius called for a 20% pullback before re-entering
- WTI crude remained below $60 per barrel on oversupply concerns and relief over delayed Iran military action
- Brent traded around $64 with consensus viewing sub-$50 oil as unlikely despite oversupply narrative

The combination of strong earnings momentum, policy uncertainty, and shifting Fed expectations continues to drive tactical rotations across asset classes.
