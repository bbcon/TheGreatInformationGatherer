**US manufacturing expanded for the first time since 2022 in January**, driving equities near record highs while **gold extended its historic three-day selloff** amid speculation over Kevin Warsh's potential Fed chairmanship and reduced policy easing expectations.

**Fed & Monetary Policy**

The ISM Manufacturing index showed unexpected expansion in January, complicating the Fed's policy path as markets reduce rate cut expectations through 2026.

- ISM Manufacturing expanded for first time since 2022, with order backlogs and export orders rising
- 10-year Treasury yields rose 4bp, approaching 4.30% as traders pare Fed cut expectations
- Kevin Warsh's increasingly likely Fed chairmanship raises questions about balance sheet policy and forward guidance
- Morgan Stanley warns of increased bond volatility under Warsh due to less transparent communications
- Fed balance sheet at $6.6 trillion, down from $9 trillion pandemic peak; Warsh historically opposed QE
- Government shutdown began Saturday, delaying JOLTS and other key labor market data releases

**Equities & Corporate Developments**

US equities rallied broadly with the S&P 500 falling just 1.5 points short of a record close, though technology leadership remains concentrated.

- S&P 500 gained 0.5% to 6,976; Dow Transports hit record highs
- Nasdaq 100 up 0.5%, but most Mag 7 names haven't reached all-time highs since fall 2024
- Consumer Staples led sectors at +1.6%; Energy worst at -2%
- Memory stocks surged: SanDisk +15% (up 1,700% since Feb 2025 spinoff), Western Digital +8%
- Oracle issued $25 billion in bonds for AI data center expansion, raising concerns about capex returns vs debt loads
- Palantir trading at 142x earnings despite recent strength
- Tesla declined as European sales plunged (France -42%, Norway -88% in January)

**Commodities & Precious Metals**

Gold and silver extended their sharp reversal from recent parabolic gains, with the selloff contained to metals rather than spreading to broader risk assets.

- Gold down 8%+ over three sessions, reversing Q4 2025 rally
- Silver also plunging despite structural supply deficits
- Gold recovering toward $5,000/oz level according to some reports, though conflicting data suggests continued weakness
- Precious metals miners sold off alongside physical metals
- Chinese banks tightening gold investment controls after retail investor losses

**International Markets & Trade**

The India-US trade deal provided a significant boost to Asian markets, while China tech diverged sharply from regional strength.

- India-US tariff cut to 18% from 25-50% drove rupee rally and equity surge
- Deal includes $500 billion in US exports over five years and halt to Russian oil purchases
- MSCI Asia gained 2.8% in best day since April, led by Samsung +10% and Kospi +6%
- Hang Seng Tech fell 3%, approaching bear market territory at -20% from October highs
- Combined Samsung/SK Hynix market cap now exceeds Tencent and Alibaba
- China PMI returned to contraction in January after December improvement
- Indonesian rupiah at Asian Financial Crisis lows versus dollar
- RBA delivered rate hike but maintained cautious tone

**Currency & Fixed Income**

The dollar strengthened for a second consecutive day as debasement trades unwound and manufacturing strength supported higher rate expectations.

- Bloomberg Dollar Spot Index gained 0.3%, reversing recent debasement positioning
- EUR/USD at 1.1815 ahead of ECB decision Thursday
- French inflation at 0.4% YoY, a five-year low well below ECB's 2% target
- 30-year Treasury yields reached 492bp, with back-end steepening continuing
- Yield curve steepening trade gaining traction on potential Warsh Fed policies

**Key Risks & Outlook**

Several developments warrant close monitoring as markets navigate policy uncertainty and stretched valuations in certain sectors.

- AI capex sustainability concerns as Oracle's $25 billion bond issuance highlights debt-funded expansion risks
- SpaceX-XAI merger at $1.25 trillion valuation appears elevated given XAI's reported $1 billion monthly cash burn
- Precious metals volatility spillover risk, though selloff remains contained to metals complex
- China EV regulatory changes including ban on hidden door handles affecting 60% of top-selling models
- Potential government funding resolution as Trump urges Republican support for stopgap measures
