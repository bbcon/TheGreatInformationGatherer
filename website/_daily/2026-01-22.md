U.S. equities rallied sharply as President Trump dialed back tariff threats against Europe and announced a "framework deal" on Greenland, though defensive positioning persists with gold at records despite the risk-on move.

**Equities & Earnings**

Markets rebounded from yesterday's selloff as geopolitical tensions eased, though the recovery remained incomplete. 

- S&P 500 gained 1.1% (reached session highs of +1.6%) but failed to fully recoup prior losses
- Russell 2000 surged 1.8%, extending its outperformance streak to 13 consecutive sessions
- Nasdaq 100 advanced 1.6%, led by semiconductors (+3%) while software declined for the seventh straight day
- Q4 earnings breadth continues improving with 9 of 11 sectors now showing positive growth versus only 2 in Q2
- Intel jumped 12% ahead of earnings, reaching four-year highs
- Energy led sectors (+2.4%) on natural gas surge; utilities lagged (+0.2%) as investors rotated from defensives

**Fed & Monetary Policy**

Trump narrowed the Fed Chair race while maintaining pressure on current leadership.

- Trump identified Rieder and Warsh as "good options" for Fed Chair, with announcement potentially coming next week
- Supreme Court hearing on Cook firing showed justices reluctant to resolve constitutional issues, likely keeping Fed as political target
- Trump on Powell: "If he won't leave, I won't make his life happy"
- Jobless claims rose to 200K (below 209K estimate), maintaining tight labor market conditions
- Final Q3 GDP confirmed 4.4% growth, beating estimates

**Bonds & Currency**

Treasury yields declined modestly despite risk-on sentiment, reflecting foreign diversification away from U.S. assets.

- 10-year yields fell 4bps as TCW noted "quiet quitting" of U.S. bonds by foreign buyers
- VIX collapsed 3 points to 16 handle as systematic strategies covered shorts
- Dollar rebound remained muted despite tariff de-escalation
- Deutsche Bank notes discretionary investors have remained neutral for 6-8 months

**Tariffs & Trade Policy**

Trump's policy reversals continued the pattern of initial threats followed by market-friendly walk-backs.

- European tariffs scheduled for February 1st were canceled after "framework deal" announcement
- Canadian election dynamics shifted due to Trump rhetoric, with 30% drop in Canadian visitors to U.S.
- Credit card rate cap proposal pivoted to seeking Congressional authority rather than executive order
- Jamie Dimon warned 80% of Americans could lose credit access if 10% cap implemented

**Economic Data & Consumer**

Mixed housing signals and energy price spikes highlighted economic crosscurrents.

- Pending home sales fell most since April 2020 despite mortgage applications at 2023 highs
- Bank of America consumer spending data showed +5% year-over-year growth in Q4, accelerating in January
- Natural gas futures jumped 27% (biggest one-day gain since 2022) on East Coast cold snap
- Q4 GDP tracking remains robust at 5.3% per Fed GDPNow

**Commodities & Alternatives**

Gold continued defensive bid despite equity rally, while private market pressures build.

- Goldman raised gold target to $5,600/oz from $4,800, citing institutional and retail demand joining central banks
- Private equity sits on $5 trillion in locked-up equity, creating potential forced-selling pressure as 2018-2020 deals mature
- EQT announced $3.2 billion acquisition of Collar Capital as secondaries market activity surges

**Corporate Developments**

Earnings season continues with mixed results highlighting valuation concerns.

- GE Aerospace beat estimates but rally faded
- Netflix declined 2% on spending guidance despite subscriber growth
- Software multiples at 99th percentile historically per UBS, raising bar for upcoming results
- Microsoft and Apple earnings next week represent critical tests for mega-cap tech valuations
- Alibaba preparing IPO for Tea Head chip unit amid ongoing AI investment theme

**Market Structure**

Systematic positioning suggests continued volatility despite recent calm.

- Small caps benefit from earnings broadening and neutral institutional positioning
- Software sector's seven-day decline marks longest losing streak since 2022
- Semiconductor strength contrasts with broader tech weakness, suggesting rotation within sector
- Senior housing/healthcare REITs gaining attention as 10,000 boomers turn 80 daily
