DAILY BRIEF:
US Treasury yields fell as markets digested Friday's benign January CPI data, with the 10-year yield dropping below 4.03% as traders priced in a potential third Fed cut this year. Separately, Iran-US nuclear talks commenced in Geneva while Iranian Revolutionary Guards conducted military exercises in the Strait of Hormuz. BHP reported its strongest earnings from copper operations, with the metal contributing over 50% of EBITDA for the first time as prices surged 33%.

**Fed & Monetary Policy**

Markets continued repricing Fed policy following Friday's CPI data, with traders now pricing a potential third rate cut this year versus the prior consensus of two cuts.

- 10-year Treasury yield fell to 4.03%, down approximately 2 basis points Tuesday in a continued rally
- 2-year yield at 3.39%, 30-year at 4.67% as safety bid persists
- Kevin Warsh widely expected as next Fed chair, raising concerns his communication approach could add volatility and require higher term premium
- Fed minutes due Friday alongside PCE data amid elevated policy uncertainty

**UK Economic Data & BOE Outlook**

UK labor market weakness intensified ahead of Wednesday's CPI release, cementing expectations for aggressive BOE easing.

- Unemployment rose to a 5-year high of 4.4%, with negative job creation over the past year
- Wage growth slowed to 4.2% versus 4.6% expected, though still above BOE's inflation-consistent target of 3.25%
- Youth unemployment (18-24 males) reached 17%, highlighting structural labor market weakness
- Sterling fell below 1.36 on weak data, with April BOE cut now fully priced and 70%+ odds for March
- Wednesday CPI expected at 3.0% from 3.4% prior - key confirmation for dovish pivot

**Geopolitical Developments**

Iran-US nuclear negotiations began in Geneva with Oman mediation, creating binary outcomes for oil markets.

- Iran signaling flexibility on uranium enrichment if US lifts sanctions
- Revolutionary Guards conducting military drills in Strait of Hormuz with threatening language toward US forces
- WTI crude up 1% on talk uncertainty despite potential for increased supply if sanctions lifted
- Brent trading at $68/barrel, down 0.6%, with geopolitical premium intact
- Russia-Ukraine talks also underway in Geneva though major sticking points remain unresolved

**Equities & Corporate Earnings**

US equity futures declined as markets reopened after Presidents Day holiday, with positioning concerns weighing on sentiment.

- S&P 500 futures down 0.5% in thin post-holiday trading
- NASDAQ 100 leading declines amid ongoing AI software valuation concerns
- VIX elevated at approximately 22, with volatility expected to persist given heavy positioning
- Small caps may test 50-day moving average as rotation from 2024 winners continues
- Asian markets closed for Lunar New Year; hedge funds reportedly built 10%+ stakes in Samsung, SK Hynix, and TSMC

**Commodities & Materials**

BHP delivered standout earnings driven by copper's structural rally, highlighting the metal's supply-demand dynamics.

- Copper contributed over 50% of BHP's EBITDA for the first time as realized prices rose ~33%
- Company raising guidance for Escondida mine operations in constructive price environment
- BHP shares gained 5% in Sydney trading on earnings beat
- Additional revenue of $2 billion from byproduct gold, silver, and uranium operations

**Japan Markets**

Japanese government bonds rallied strongly as fiscal concerns from January fully unwound.

- JGBs gained 10-12 basis points in the long end following strong 5-year auction
- Yen strengthened modestly on haven flows amid geopolitical uncertainty
- Bank of Japan-PM meeting signaled no government interference in monetary policy
- Kishida administration making "right noises" on fiscal discipline

**Key Risks & Catalysts**

Several binary outcomes loom this week that could drive significant market moves.

- Iran nuclear talk outcomes creating oil volatility with sanctions relief versus supply disruption scenarios
- UK CPI Wednesday critical for BOE policy path given wage growth still elevated
- US PCE Friday alongside Fed minutes amid leadership transition uncertainty
- German ZEW survey due 10:00 GMT as European sentiment gauge
