**Weekly Macro Brief: February 9-13, 2026**

Markets faced a week of conflicting signals as Japan's historic election victory triggered policy uncertainty, AI disruption fears broadened across sectors, and mixed US economic data left Fed policy expectations in flux. Asian equities reached record highs early in the week before succumbing to global technology selloffs, while dollar weakness persisted despite stronger-than-expected jobs data.

**Japan Policy Revolution and Yen Intervention Risks**

Prime Minister Takaichi's landslide election victory delivered the largest mandate since WWII, fundamentally altering Japan's policy landscape and triggering unprecedented market moves.

- Nikkei surged 4% on Monday to record highs, extending gains through Tuesday with a 2.8% rise
- LDP secured two-thirds supermajority, sufficient to override upper house vetoes on constitutional changes
- Proposed sales tax suspension on food worth Â¥5 trillion ($31B annually) despite claims of no additional JGB issuance needed
- USD/JPY fell sharply to 156 on Monday, then continued declining to 152 by Friday despite stronger US data

Central bank policy expectations shifted dramatically as markets repriced BOJ normalization timeline.

- April hike probability jumped to 80% from previous June base case
- Rate expectations rose to 100bp from 75bp as officials suggested current 0.75% may be "too accommodative"
- 40-year JGB yields dropped 10bp on Thursday to 3.625%, signaling long-end volatility
- MOF intervention warnings intensified with officials citing "high sense of urgency" around FX moves

**AI Disruption Fears Reach Critical Mass**

What began as software sector concerns metastasized into broader market disruption fears, triggering the week's most significant equity moves.

Technology sectors faced mounting pressure as AI displacement narratives spread beyond traditional targets.

- Software ETF posted fifth consecutive weekly decline, falling 3% on Friday alone
- Wealth management firms hit hard: Charles Schwab down 7.4%, Raymond James declined 8.8% on AI tax planning tools
- Cisco plunged 13% on Friday citing margin pressure from DRAM costs, triggering hardware selloffs
- Asian markets finally succumbed Friday, with Tencent falling 22% from recent highs

However, AI infrastructure demand remained robust, creating sector bifurcation.

- TSMC January sales surged 37% year-over-year to record levels
- Memory chip shortages intensified with DRAM prices up 600% recently
- Applied Materials gained 11% after hours Friday on strong earnings validating chip demand
- Hyperscaler spending reached $650B annual run rate, up 60% year-over-year

**US Economic Data Confusion and Fed Policy Flux**

Mixed economic signals left markets struggling to establish coherent Fed policy expectations as data painted conflicting pictures of US economic health.

December retail sales disappointed broadly on Wednesday, reinforcing dovish expectations.

- Headline growth flat at 0% versus modest expansion expectations
- 8 of 13 retail categories declined month-over-month
- Control group fell 0.1%, signaling underlying consumer weakness
- Markets repriced to three Fed cuts by year-end from two previously

Thursday's jobs data provided sharp contradiction, doubling payroll expectations.

- 130,000 jobs added versus 65,000 consensus, pushing unemployment to 4.3%
- Fed cut expectations pushed back to July from June, though 2-3 cuts remain priced for 2026
- Treasury yields surged across the curve before Friday's CPI-driven rally

January CPI on Friday met expectations but kept cuts on the table.

- Core inflation 2.5% year-over-year, 0.3% month-over-month as expected
- Shelter inflation showed meaningful deceleration after pandemic stickiness
- Tariff effects visible in appliances (+1.3%) and computers (+3.1%)
- Markets now price 58bp of cuts by year-end, up from 40bp two weeks prior

**Asian Market Outperformance Reversal**

Asia-Pacific equities posted their best year-to-date start this century before succumbing to global technology fears in Friday's session.

Regional strength built throughout the week on multiple supportive factors.

- MSCI Asia-Pacific outperformed S&P 500 by 10 percentage points year-to-date
- Korea's Kospi hit fresh highs with Samsung and SK Hynix each gaining 3-4% Thursday
- These two stocks account for 51% of the Kospi's gains, highlighting concentration risk
- Indonesia's 70% nickel production cut supported battery material stocks

Currency dynamics supported the regional rally as dollar weakness persisted.

- Offshore yuan approached 6.90 handle, marking longest winning streak in two years
- Eight consecutive daily gains brought USD/CNY to 6.9398 by Friday
- PBOC allowed appreciation despite potential deflationary pressure
- Asian equity gains finally reversed Friday as AI disruption fears reached the region

**Bond Markets and Credit Appetite**

Fixed income markets demonstrated resilience amid equity volatility, with credit appetite remaining robust despite technology sector concerns.

Alphabet's $20 billion global bond issuance on Tuesday exceeded initial $15 billion targets.

- Included unprecedented 100-year sterling note at 95bp spread over Treasuries
- AI-related issuance approached 20% of investment-grade index
- Investment-grade spreads reached 2007 lows despite equity market skepticism

Treasury markets provided traditional hedge function during Friday's selloff.

- 10-year yields fell 7bp Friday, dropping below 4.10% at session lows
- 2-year yields hit 3.43%, lowest since September 2022
- 30-year auction showed record end-user demand with lowest dealer takedown at 4.73%
- Bull flattening reflected classic flight-to-safety flows
