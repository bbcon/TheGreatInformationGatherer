The S&P 500 touched 7,000 for the first time as Meta surged 8% on record capex guidance, while Microsoft plunged 7% on slowing Azure growth despite elevated spending. Markets showed stark divergence between AI winners and losers as single-stock volatility hit historic highs.

What moved markets today, by order of importance.

Fed & Monetary Policy

The Federal Reserve held rates at 4.25-4.50% as expected, but two governors dissented for cuts - Waller and Myron - marking unexpected dovish pressure within the committee.

• Powell defended Fed independence amid ongoing Trump criticism, stating "we haven't lost it, strongly committed"

• Markets pushed first rate cut expectations to September from earlier timeline

• Trump preparing Kevin Walsh Fed chair nomination announcement for Friday, sources indicate

• Walsh viewed as more hawkish than alternatives despite recent dovish rhetoric from his 2006-2011 Fed tenure

Equities & Earnings

Technology earnings revealed sharp performance divergence as markets shifted from "rising tide" to "winners and losers" phase in AI spending.

• **Meta:** +8% on $135 billion capex guidance (+87% YoY), justified by record advertising revenue growth in holiday quarter

• **Microsoft:** -7% as Azure cloud growth of 38% lagged capex increase of 66%, raising capital allocation concerns

• **Apple:** $144 billion revenue (+16%) with $25 billion China sales (first growth in 4 years), but gains faded on unclear AI strategy

• **Tesla:** Announced $20 billion capex (2x estimates) for AI/robotics and potential in-house semiconductor foundry

• **Software sector collapse:** SAP -15%, ServiceNow -8% on AI disruption fears and cloud weakness

• **Memory/storage surge:** Seagate +20%, SanDisk posting 51% gross margins on supply shortages

Single-stock volatility reached historic highs as big tech correlation dropped to 20-25% from prior 40-50% levels.

Commodities & FX

Precious metals hit new records on dollar weakness and debasement fears, while base metals rallied on AI infrastructure demand.

• **Gold:** New all-time high above $5,500, up 28% year-to-date on 9-day rally

• **Silver:** Record above $120/oz, up 60% year-to-date

• **Copper:** 16-year high at $13,942/ton on supply deficit of 300K-800K tons in 26 million ton market

• **Oil:** Brent +6% to $70 on Iran military escalation threats from Trump administration

• **Dollar:** 4-year lows despite Treasury Secretary Bessent's "strong dollar" rhetoric, with EUR/USD at 1.19

Corporate Developments

OpenAI raised $40 billion in new funding amid existential pressure as the only frontier AI model without distribution channel.

• Nvidia contributing $20 billion, Amazon $10 billion, Microsoft several billion more

• Company burning $9 billion monthly according to reports

• Now advertising for first time, previously viewed as "desperate" signal

• Amazon separately in talks for $50 billion investment valuing OpenAI at $830 billion

Geopolitical & Risk Factors

Iran tensions escalated as Trump threatened military strikes "with speed and violence" if no nuclear deal reached.

• US carrier fleet deployed to region supporting oil rally

• Government shutdown risk over weekend as Senate/White House near DHS funding deal

• Indonesia markets in crisis with 8% intraday drop before recovering, facing potential MSCI downgrade to frontier status

Market Structure

Retail investors showed shifting options behavior, reducing aggressive call buying while increasing call selling (overwriting strategies).

• Small cap call buying reached 90th percentile as rotation from mega-cap AI exposure accelerated

• Memory chip supply constraints creating 2-year lag before new fab capacity comes online

• CEO turnover accelerating globally, up 16% year-over-year with average tenure declining to 7.1 years
